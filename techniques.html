🧠 Productivity Techniques

Pomodoro Technique 🍅

Work for 25 minutes, then take a 5-minute break. After four cycles, take a 15–30 minute long break. Improves focus and prevents burnout.

Time Blocking 📅

Elon Musk and Bill Gates use this method. Block out your entire day by assigning tasks to specific hours, leaving little to no “free” time.

Eisenhower Matrix 🧮

Divide tasks into four categories: Urgent & Important, Important but Not Urgent, Urgent but Not Important, Neither. Helps in making decisions fast.

Deep Work 🧘

Coined by Cal Newport. Block all distractions and focus intensely on one high-impact task for 1–2 hours.

2-Minute Rule ⏱️

If a task takes less than two minutes, do it now. Helps eliminate tiny procrastinations that slow down your flow.

Eat That Frog 🐸

From Brian Tracy. Do your hardest or most important task first thing in the morning. It builds momentum for the day.

MIT (Most Important Task) 🧩

Identify and focus on 1–3 high-priority tasks that will have the biggest impact. Used by productivity experts like Leo Babauta (Zen Habits).

80/20 Rule (Pareto Principle) 📊

80% of results come from 20% of efforts. Identify and focus on high-impact activities to maximize productivity.

Don’t Break the Chain ✅

Popularized by Jerry Seinfeld. Mark an “X” on the calendar for each day you complete your task. Keep the streak alive!

Single-Tasking 🎯

Focus on one task at a time instead of multitasking. This leads to better quality work and faster completion.

Bullet Journaling 📝

Combine task management, journaling, and habit tracking in a single place. Excellent for mindful productivity.

SMART Goals 🎯

Set goals that are Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound. Keeps you focused and accountable.

5-Second Rule 🚀

From Mel Robbins. Count “5–4–3–2–1” and take action. Helps overcome hesitation and procrastination quickly.

Daily Review & Planning 🧾

Spend 10 minutes reviewing what you did today and planning tomorrow. Builds reflection and control over your time.
